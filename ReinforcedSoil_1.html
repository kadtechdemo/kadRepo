Product Features

World leader in Reinforced Earth MSE wall technology

Our diverse choice of solutions includes a practical selection of facing elements and soil reinforcements that are configured to best meet your specific project requirements.

Choice of slope soil reinforcement

Geosynthetic soil reinforcements come in the form of either geostrips (GeoStrap® and EcoStrap®) or ArmaGrid® high-permance geogrids.

GeoStrap® reinforcements are made of high tenacity polyester (PET) tendons contained in a polyethylene sheath. This soil reinforcement is well adapted in the presence of chlorides sulphates and low pH environments.

EcoStrap® reinforcements are made of polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) tendons contained in a polyethylene sheath. This soil reinforcement is well adapted in the presence of recycled concrete, lime or cement and high pH environments.

ArmaGrid® uniaxial geogrid reinforcement is manufactured in both PET and polypropylene (PP), and both are ideal for building both permanent and temporary reinforced soil slopes.

Choice of slope facing

We provide both steel wire mesh facing and geosynthetic facing solutions. These choices allow to build either ArmaGreen® vegetated “green” slopes, or ArmaStone® slopes having a natural mineral appearance. We also offer a popular alternative to build using the ArmaWeb® expandable geosynthetic cellular soil confinement solution which is highly adaptable for vegetation.

Applications

Slopes are also frequently built to support and protect linear infrastructure including roadways, railways and airports and are commonly built for waste containment at landfills for both startup and landfill expansions.

In addition, RSS and GRS slopes are needed to control natural gravitational risks such as rockfall, landslides, avalanches and hydraulic erosion and coastal and river flood protection.

Steep slopes are also constructed to address anthropogenic risks such as explosions, assault, noise and traffic protection.
